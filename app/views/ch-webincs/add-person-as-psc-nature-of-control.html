Nature of control Joe Bloggs

The nature of control is what gives the person their influence over the company.

Which nature of control should I choose?

For most small companies, only the following are likely to apply:

  • ownership of shares as a person
  • ownership of voting rights as a person
  • the person has the right to appoint or remove the majority of the board of directors of the company

If none of the above apply then the following option may be more suitable:

  • the person has significant influence or control over the company

A small company with a straightforward structure is not likely to use the options relating to a member of a firm or trustee of a trust.
